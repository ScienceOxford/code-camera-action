CCC - Code, Camera, Action!

Resources for the 'Code, Camera, Action!' Creative Computing Club.

During this workshop, you will program micro:bits to act out a story of your own design, film it, and edit your video using Kinemaster.

This page recaps what we discussed as a group.

To skip to the examples and ideas, click here.


Getting started

Using the microbit

Use the display.scroll() function to make the microbit scroll some text.

Think about your story and what you want your character to say.

Now add a while True loop and the line if button_a.is_pressed(): so that the text scrolls when you press the A button

Using Kinemaster

Create a new project in Kinemaster, close the Media browser and find the red camera button on the right of the screen.

Press it and select 'camcorder' - now you're ready to record a video!

When you're ready, press record and then press the microbit's A button to make it scroll the tex.t

Once you've recorded it, you can drag the edge of the clip on your timeline to crop out the start with your hand.

Now record some more clips - Change the microbit's position and/or change what it says.

Try adding a transition between clips to make it look interesting!
